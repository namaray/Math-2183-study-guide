Gaussian vs. Gauss-Jordan Elimination

A comparative guide to the two fundamental algorithms for solving linear systems.

1. Gaussian Elimination

Goal: Transform the matrix into Row Echelon Form (REF).

This method stops "halfway" through the reduction process. Once the matrix is in REF, you solve the system using Back-Substitution.

Target: Row Echelon Form (REF)
$$ \begin{bmatrix} 1 & * & * & | & * \\ 0 & 1 & * & | & * \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & | & * \end{bmatrix} $$ (The $*$ represents any number. Zeros are only required below the main diagonal.)

The Process

Step 1: Forward Elimination
Use row operations to create zeros below the pivots (leading 1s) starting from the first column.
Step 2: Back-Substitution
Convert the final matrix back into equations. Solve for the last variable ($z$), substitute it into the previous equation to find $y$, and so on.

2. Gauss-Jordan Elimination

Goal: Transform the matrix into Reduced Row Echelon Form (RREF).

This method continues the reduction until the variable values can be "read" directly from the matrix without any further algebra.

Target: Reduced Row Echelon Form (RREF)
$$ \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 & | & a \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & | & b \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & | & c \end{bmatrix} $$ (The solution is immediately visible: $x=a, y=b, z=c$.)

The Process

Step 1: Forward Elimination
Same as Gaussian Elimination. Get zeros below the pivots.
Step 2: Normalize Pivots
Ensure every pivot is exactly equal to 1 (divide the row by the pivot value).
Step 3: Backward Elimination
Use row operations to create zeros above the pivots as well. This isolates the variable in each column.

3. Comparison & Differences

Feature Gaussian Elimination Gauss-Jordan Elimination
Final Matrix Form Row Echelon Form (REF)
(Upper Triangular)		 Reduced Row Echelon Form (RREF)
(Diagonal/Identity)
Operations Zeros below diagonal only. Zeros below AND above diagonal.
Next Step Requires Back-Substitution. Solution is immediate.
Efficiency More Efficient (approx 50% fewer operations). Preferred for computers. Less Efficient. Preferred for hand calculation or finding Inverses.
Key Takeaway: Gaussian Elimination is faster computationally, but requires an extra algebra step at the end. Gauss-Jordan takes longer to reduce the matrix but gives the answer explicitly.
