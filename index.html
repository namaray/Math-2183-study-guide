1. Differential Equations

To solve non-homogeneous linear equations with constant coefficients[cite: 137, 138]:

$$a_n y^{(n)} + a_{n-1} y^{(n-1)} + \dots + a_1 y' + a_0 y = g(x)$$ [cite: 147]

Homogeneous Part ($y_c$)

Solve the auxiliary equation: $am^2 + bm + c = 0$[cite: 251, 257].

  • Distinct Real Roots: $y = C_1e^{m_1x} + C_2e^{m_2x}$[cite: 265].
  • Repeated Real Roots: $y = C_1e^{m_1x} + C_2xe^{m_1x}$[cite: 266].
  • Complex Roots: $y = e^{\alpha x}(C_1 \cos \beta x + C_2 \sin \beta x)$[cite: 287].

2. Linear Systems & Rank

Consistency rules based on the rank of the coefficient matrix ($r_A$) and augmented matrix ($r_{Ab}$)[cite: 521, 910, 911]:

  • No Solution: $rank(A) \neq rank([A|b])$[cite: 527, 912].
  • Unique Solution: $rank(A) = rank([A|b]) = n$[cite: 531, 917].
  • Infinite Solutions: $rank(A) = rank([A|b]) < n$[cite: 535, 914, 915].

3. Gaussian Elimination

Used to transform an augmented matrix into Row-Echelon Form (REF)[cite: 553, 928].

Row Operations[cite: 556, 930]:

  1. $R_i \leftarrow R_i - cR_j$ (Subtract a multiple of one row from another).
  2. $R_i \leftrightarrow R_j$ (Interchange two rows).
  3. $R_i \leftarrow cR_i$ (Multiply a row by a non-zero constant).

The rank is equal to the number of non-zero rows in the REF[cite: 559, 935].

4. Gauss-Jordan Method

An extension of Gaussian elimination to achieve Reduced Row-Echelon Form (RREF).

Goal: Each leading 1 is the only non-zero entry in its column. This allows you to solve for variables directly without back-substitution.

5. Worked Examples

Linear System Row Reduction (Prob 13.i)

System: $x-y+2z=3$, $3x+y-2z=0$, $-x+2y-z=-1$[cite: 51].

Augmented Matrix: $$\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & 2 & 3 \\ 3 & 1 & -2 & 0 \\ -1 & 2 & -1 & -1 \end{array}\right]$$ First Operations: $R_2 \leftarrow R_2 - 3R_1$ and $R_3 \leftarrow R_3 + R_1$: $$\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & 2 & 3 \\ 0 & 4 & -8 & -9 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 2 \end{array}\right]$$

Differential Equations (Prob 1)

Solve: $y'' + 3y' + 2y = 6$[cite: 15].

Homogeneous Solution ($y_c$):
$m^2 + 3m + 2 = 0 \implies m = -1, -2 \implies y_c = C_1e^{-x} + C_2e^{-2x}$.

Particular Solution ($y_p$):
Let $y_p = A$. Then $2A = 6 \implies A = 3$.

General Solution: $y = C_1e^{-x} + C_2e^{-2x} + 3$.
