7. Invertibility & Determinants

A matrix is invertible if $\det(A) \neq 0$[cite: 92].

Prob 17.a: $A = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 1 \\ 5 & 2 \end{bmatrix}$
$\det(A) = (6 \cdot 2) - (1 \cdot 5) = 7$
$A^{-1} = \frac{1}{7} \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ -5 & 6 \end{bmatrix}$ [cite: 93]
Prob 17.b: $A = \begin{bmatrix} -1 & 2 \\ 3 & -6 \end{bmatrix}$
$\det(A) = (-1 \cdot -6) - (2 \cdot 3) = 6 - 6 = 0$
Result: Singular / Not Invertible [cite: 93]

9. Verification of Inverses

Confirming $AB = BA = I$[cite: 101].

10. 3x3 Matrix Analysis

Detailed analysis for $A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 3 \\ 2 & 5 & 3 \\ 1 & 0 & 8 \end{bmatrix}$[cite: 109].

1. Determinant Expansion (Row 3):
$\det(A) = 1(6-15) - 0 + 8(5-4) = -9 + 8 = -1$ [cite: 104]
2. Trace Calculation:
$tr(A) = tr(A^T) = 1 + 5 + 8 = 14$ [cite: 107]
3. Inverse Matrix:
$A^{-1} = \begin{bmatrix} -40 & 16 & 9 \\ 13 & -5 & -3 \\ 5 & -2 & -1 \end{bmatrix}$ [cite: 109]
