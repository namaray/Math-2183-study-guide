7. Invertibility & Determinants

A matrix is invertible if $\det(A) \neq 0$[cite: 92].

Prob 17.a: $A = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 1 \\ 5 & 2 \end{bmatrix}$
$\det(A) = (6 \cdot 2) - (1 \cdot 5) = 7$
$A^{-1} = \frac{1}{7} \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ -5 & 6 \end{bmatrix}$ [cite: 93]
Prob 17.b: $A = \begin{bmatrix} -1 & 2 \\ 3 & -6 \end{bmatrix}$
$\det(A) = (-1 \cdot -6) - (2 \cdot 3) = 6 - 6 = 0$
Result: Singular / Not Invertible [cite: 93]

4. Finding the Infinite Solutions (Problem 6)

Based on the rank analysis ($\text{rank} < n$), we determined the system has infinitely many solutions. Here is the procedure to express them using parameters.

Step 1: Recall the Reduced Row Echelon Form (RREF)

From the previous row operations, the augmented matrix simplified to:

$$[A|b] = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 2 & | & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & | & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & | & 0 \end{bmatrix}$$

Step 2: Write the Equation

The only surviving non-zero row gives us a single equation:

$$x + y + 2z = 1$$

Step 3: Identify Variables

  • Pivot Variable ($x$): Corresponds to the leading "1" in the first column.
  • Free Variables ($y, z$): Correspond to columns without leading "1"s.

Step 4: Solve in Parametric Form

We assign arbitrary parameters to the free variables (where $s, t \in \mathbb{R}$):

  • Let $y = s$
  • Let $z = t$

Now, rearrange the equation from Step 2 to solve for the pivot variable $x$:

$$x = 1 - y - 2z$$ $$x = 1 - s - 2t$$

Step 5: Final Solution Vector

Combine the components into a single vector, then decompose it to separate the constants and parameters:

General Solution: $$\begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \\ z \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 - s - 2t \\ s \\ t \end{bmatrix}$$
Vector Decomposition: $$\begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \\ z \end{bmatrix} = \underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}}_{\text{Particular Sol.}} + s \underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}}_{\text{Basis Vector 1}} + t \underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} -2 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}}_{\text{Basis Vector 2}}$$
