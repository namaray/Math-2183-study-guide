7. Invertibility & Determinants

A matrix is invertible if $\det(A) \neq 0$[cite: 92].

Prob 17.a: $A = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 1 \\ 5 & 2 \end{bmatrix}$
$\det(A) = (6 \cdot 2) - (1 \cdot 5) = 7$
$A^{-1} = \frac{1}{7} \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ -5 & 6 \end{bmatrix}$ [cite: 93]
Prob 17.b: $A = \begin{bmatrix} -1 & 2 \\ 3 & -6 \end{bmatrix}$
$\det(A) = (-1 \cdot -6) - (2 \cdot 3) = 6 - 6 = 0$
Result: Singular / Not Invertible [cite: 93]

Problem 6: Complete Solution

Detailed breakdown of Rank Analysis and finding the General Solution for the given system.

Part 1: Rank Analysis & Consistency

Given System:

$$A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 2 \\ 2 & 2 & 4 \\ 3 & 3 & 6 \end{bmatrix}, \quad b = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix}$$

Step 1: Row Reduction to REF

We form the augmented matrix $[A|b]$ and perform row operations to zero out the entries below the first pivot.

Initial Matrix:

$$[A|b] = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 2 & | & 1 \\ 2 & 2 & 4 & | & 2 \\ 3 & 3 & 6 & | & 3 \end{bmatrix}$$

Operations:

  • $R_2 \to R_2 - 2R_1$
  • $R_3 \to R_3 - 3R_1$

Calculation:

  • Row 2: $[2-2, 2-2, 4-4 \mid 2-2] \to [0, 0, 0 \mid 0]$
  • Row 3: $[3-3, 3-3, 6-6 \mid 3-3] \to [0, 0, 0 \mid 0]$

Resulting Matrix (REF):

$$\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 2 & | & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & | & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & | & 0 \end{bmatrix}$$

Step 2: Determine Ranks

  • rank(A): Number of non-zero rows in $A$ part = 1.
  • rank([A|b]): Number of non-zero rows in total matrix = 1.
  • n (Unknowns): Variables $x, y, z$ = 3.

Conclusion on Consistency

Since $\text{rank}(A) = \text{rank}([A|b]) = 1$, the system is Consistent.

Since the rank ($r=1$) is less than the number of unknowns ($n=3$), there are:

$$n - r = 3 - 1 = 2 \text{ Free Variables}$$

Result: Infinitely Many Solutions.

Part 2: Finding the General Solution

We now solve for the variables using the Reduced Row Echelon Form found above.

Step 3: Write the Equation

From the first row $[1 \quad 1 \quad 2 \quad | \quad 1]$, we get:

$$x + y + 2z = 1$$

Step 4: Identify Variables & Parametrize

Pivot Variable: $x$ (Leading 1)

Free Variables: $y, z$ (No pivots)

Assign parameters to free variables (where $s, t \in \mathbb{R}$):

  • $y = s$
  • $z = t$

Step 5: Solve for Pivot Variable

Rearrange the equation from Step 3:

$$x = 1 - y - 2z$$

Substitute the parameters:

$$x = 1 - s - 2t$$

Final Solution Vector

The general solution is:

$$\begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \\ z \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 - s - 2t \\ s \\ t \end{bmatrix}$$

Decomposed Vector Form:

$$\begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \\ z \end{bmatrix} = \underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}}_{\text{Particular}} + s \underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}}_{\text{Basis 1}} + t \underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} -2 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}}_{\text{Basis 2}}$$
