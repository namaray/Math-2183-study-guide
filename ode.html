Ordinary Differential Equations (ODEs)

How to build a General Solution based on characteristic roots ($m$).

1. The Construction Rules

The general solution $y$ is formed by summing the contributions of each root:

  • Rule 1: Distinct Real Roots ($m$)
    Each root gives: $c \cdot e^{mx}$
  • Rule 2: Repeated Real Roots ($m$ appearing $k$ times)
    Gives: $(c_1 + c_2x + \dots + c_kx^{k-1})e^{mx}$
  • Rule 3: Complex Conjugate Roots ($\alpha \pm i\beta$)
    Gives: $e^{\alpha x}(c_A \cos \beta x + c_B \sin \beta x)$

2. Worksheet Solutions (Prob 13)

Covers: Repeated + Distinct Roots

Problem 13.i: Roots are $3, 3, -5$

Step 1: Handle repeated root $3$. It repeats twice, so: $(c_1 + c_2x)e^{3x}$.
Step 2: Handle distinct root $-5$. It gives: $c_3e^{-5x}$.

General Solution:
$y = (c_1 + c_2x)e^{3x} + c_3e^{-5x}$
Covers: Repeated + Complex Roots

Problem 13.ii: Roots are $3, 3$ and $1 \pm i\sqrt{3}$

Step 1: Handle repeated root $3$. It gives: $(c_1 + c_2x)e^{3x}$.
Step 2: Handle complex roots $1 \pm i\sqrt{3}$. Here, $\alpha = 1$ and $\beta = \sqrt{3}$.
It gives: $e^{1x}(c_3 \cos \sqrt{3}x + c_4 \sin \sqrt{3}x)$.

General Solution:
$y = (c_1 + c_2x)e^{3x} + e^{x}(c_3 \cos \sqrt{3}x + c_4 \sin \sqrt{3}x)$
Covers: All Three Rule Types Combined

Problem 13.iii: Roots are $-3, -3$, $1 \pm i\sqrt{3}$, and $3$

1. Repeated Roots (-3, -3): $(c_1 + c_2x)e^{-3x}$
2. Complex Roots ($1 \pm i\sqrt{3}$): $e^{x}(c_3 \cos \sqrt{3}x + c_4 \sin \sqrt{3}x)$
3. Distinct Root (3): $c_5e^{3x}$

General Solution:
$y = (c_1 + c_2x)e^{-3x} + e^{x}(c_3 \cos \sqrt{3}x + c_4 \sin \sqrt{3}x) + c_5e^{3x}$
