ODE General Solutions

Detailed breakdown of Homogeneous vs. Non-homogeneous equations and the Problem 13 worksheet solutions.

1. The Basics: Homogeneous vs. Non-homogeneous

Before solving, you must identify if the equation equals zero or a function $g(x)$.

Use this Logic:

Type Standard Form Goal
Homogeneous $ay'' + by' + cy = 0$ Find Complementary Function ($y_c$) using roots ($m$) of the characteristic equation[cite: 162].
Non-homogeneous $ay'' + by' + cy = g(x)$ Find General Solution ($y$) by adding $y_c$ (from LHS) + Particular Solution $y_p$ (from RHS)[cite: 474].

Key Formula: For non-homogeneous equations, the final answer is always:

$$y(x) = y_c(x) + y_p(x)$$

How to build $y_c$ from Roots ($m$):

  • Distinct Real Roots ($m_1 \neq m_2$): $y_c = c_1 e^{m_1 x} + c_2 e^{m_2 x}$ [cite: 165]
  • Repeated Real Roots ($m_1 = m_2 = m$): $y_c = c_1 e^{mx} + c_2 x e^{mx}$ [cite: 166]
  • Complex Roots ($\alpha \pm i\beta$): $y_c = e^{\alpha x}(c_1 \cos \beta x + c_2 \sin \beta x)$ [cite: 172]

2. Detailed Solutions for Problem 13

Below are the step-by-step constructions for the roots given in the Review Sheet [cite: 68-71].

Problem 13.i

Roots: $3, 3, -5$

Step 1: Analyze Repeated Roots ($3, 3$)
The root $m=3$ appears twice.
  • First occurrence contributes: $c_1 e^{3x}$
  • Second occurrence (multiply by $x$): $c_2 x e^{3x}$
  • Combined term: $(c_1 + c_2 x)e^{3x}$
Step 2: Analyze Distinct Root ($-5$)
The root $m=-5$ appears once.
  • Contribution: $c_3 e^{-5x}$
Final General Solution:
$$y = c_1 e^{3x} + c_2 x e^{3x} + c_3 e^{-5x}$$
Problem 13.ii

Roots: $3, 3, 1 \pm i\sqrt{3}$

Step 1: Analyze Repeated Roots ($3, 3$)
Just like above, the repeated root $3$ requires an $x$ term.
  • Contribution: $(c_1 + c_2 x)e^{3x}$
Step 2: Analyze Complex Roots ($1 \pm i\sqrt{3}$)
Identify parts: Real part $\alpha = 1$, Imaginary part $\beta = \sqrt{3}$.
Formula: $e^{\alpha x}(A \cos \beta x + B \sin \beta x)$
  • Contribution: $e^{1x}(c_3 \cos \sqrt{3}x + c_4 \sin \sqrt{3}x)$
Final General Solution:
$$y = (c_1 + c_2 x)e^{3x} + e^{x}(c_3 \cos \sqrt{3}x + c_4 \sin \sqrt{3}x)$$
Problem 13.iii

Roots: $-3, -3, 1, 1 \pm i\sqrt{3}, 3$

Note: This solution includes all roots listed in the Review Sheet text: "-3,-3, 1, 1±i√3 and 3"[cite: 71].

Step 1: Repeated Roots ($-3, -3$)
Root $-3$ repeats.
  • Contribution: $(c_1 + c_2 x)e^{-3x}$
Step 2: Distinct Real Roots ($1$ and $3$)
These are standard distinct roots.
  • For $m=1$: $c_3 e^{1x}$
  • For $m=3$: $c_4 e^{3x}$
Step 3: Complex Pair ($1 \pm i\sqrt{3}$)
$\alpha = 1, \beta = \sqrt{3}$.
  • Contribution: $e^{x}(c_5 \cos \sqrt{3}x + c_6 \sin \sqrt{3}x)$
Final General Solution:
$$y = \underbrace{(c_1 + c_2 x)e^{-3x}}_{\text{Repeated -3}} + \underbrace{c_3 e^{x} + c_4 e^{3x}}_{\text{Distinct 1 & 3}} + \underbrace{e^{x}(c_5 \cos \sqrt{3}x + c_6 \sin \sqrt{3}x)}_{\text{Complex}}$$
