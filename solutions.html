Question 13: Full Step-by-Step Solutions

Problem 13 (i)

System: $x - y + 2z = 3$, $3x + y - 2z = 0$, $-x + 2y - z = -1$[cite: 51].

Step 1: Augmented Matrix Setup
$$\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & 2 & 3 \\ 3 & 1 & -2 & 0 \\ -1 & 2 & -1 & -1 \end{array}\right]$$
Step 2: Eliminate $x$ from $R_2$ and $R_3$
$R_2 \leftarrow R_2 - 3R_1$: [3-3(1)=0, 1-3(-1)=4, -2-3(2)=-8 | 0-3(3)=-9]
$R_3 \leftarrow R_3 + R_1$: [-1+1=0, 2-1=1, -1+2=1 | -1+3=2]
$$\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & 2 & 3 \\ 0 & 4 & -8 & -9 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 2 \end{array}\right]$$
Step 3: Row Swap and Eliminate $y$ from $R_3$
Swap $R_2 \leftrightarrow R_3$, then $R_3 \leftarrow R_3 - 4R_2$: [4-4(1)=0, -8-4(1)=-12 | -9-4(2)=-17]
$$\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & 2 & 3 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 0 & -12 & -17 \end{array}\right]$$
Rank(A) = 3, Rank(Ab) = 3, n = 3. Since $r = n$, the system is Consistent with a Unique Solution [cite: 529-531, 917].

Problem 13 (ii)

System: $x - 2y + z - w = 0$, $2x - 3y + 2z + w = 0$, $3x - 2y + z - w = 0$[cite: 58].

Step 1: Row Reduction for Column 1
$R_2 \leftarrow R_2 - 2R_1$: [2-2=0, -3+4=1, 2-2=0, 1+2=3 | 0]
$R_3 \leftarrow R_3 - 3R_1$: [3-3=0, -2+6=4, 1-3=-2, -1+3=2 | 0]
Step 2: Eliminate $y$ from $R_3$
$R_3 \leftarrow R_3 - 4R_2$: [4-4(1)=0, -2-4(0)=-2, 2-4(3)=-10 | 0]
$$\left[\begin{array}{cccc|c} 1 & -2 & 1 & -1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & -2 & -10 & 0 \end{array}\right]$$
Rank(A) = 3, Rank(Ab) = 3, n = 4. Since $r < n$, the system is Consistent with Infinitely Many Solutions [cite: 533-535, 914-915].

Problem 13 (iii)

System: $x_1 + x_2 + 2x_3 = 8$, $-x_1 - 2x_2 + 3x_3 = 1$, $3x_1 - 7x_2 + 4x_3 = 10$ .

Step 1: Column 1 and 2 Reduction
$R_2 \leftarrow R_2 + R_1$: [-1+1=0, -2+1=-1, 3+2=5 | 1+8=9]
$R_3 \leftarrow R_3 - 3R_1$: [3-3=0, -7-3=-10, 4-6=-2 | 10-24=-14]
$R_3 \leftarrow R_3 - 10R_2$: [-10-10(-1)=0, -2-10(5)=-52 | -14-10(9)=-104]
Step 2: Back-substitution
1. $-52x_3 = -104 \implies x_3 = 2$
2. $-x_2 + 5(2) = 9 \implies -x_2 = -1 \implies x_2 = 1$
3. $x_1 + (1) + 2(2) = 8 \implies x_1 = 3$
Rank(A) = 3, Rank(Ab) = 3, n = 3. Unique Solution: (3, 1, 2).

Problem 13 (iv)

System: $3x_1 + 2x_2 - x_3 = -15$, $5x_1 + 3x_2 + 2x_3 = 0$, $3x_1 + x_2 + 3x_3 = 11$, $6x_1 - 4x_2 + 2x_3 = 30$ [cite: 64-67].

Step 1: Partial Row Reduction
$R_3 \leftarrow R_3 - R_1 \implies [0, -1, 4 | 26]$
$R_4 \leftarrow R_4 - 2R_1 \implies [0, -8, 4 | 60]$
$R_4 \leftarrow R_4 - 8R_3 \implies [0, 0, -28 | -148]$
Rank(A) = 3, Rank(Ab) = 4. Because $rank(A) \neq rank([A|b])$, the system is Inconsistent (No Solution) [cite: 523-524, 611, 911-912].
