Geometric Matrix Transformations

Detailed guide on how matrices map points in 2D space.

1. Standard Transformation Matrices

Every linear transformation $T(x,y)$ can be represented by a standard matrix $A$ such that $T(x) = Ax$.

Transformation Standard Matrix ($2 \times 2$) Effect on $(x, y)$
Reflection (x-axis) $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 \end{bmatrix}$ $(x, -y)$
Reflection (y-axis) $\begin{bmatrix} -1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$ $(-x, y)$
Reflection ($y=x$) $\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{bmatrix}$ $(y, x)$
Reflection ($y=-x$) $\begin{bmatrix} 0 & -1 \\ -1 & 0 \end{bmatrix}$ $(-y, -x)$
Rotation (90° CW) $\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 0 \end{bmatrix}$ $(y, -x)$
Rotation (90° ACW) $\begin{bmatrix} 0 & -1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{bmatrix}$ $(-y, x)$

2. Full Reflection Analysis (Prob 21)

Detailed calculations for $A(2,3)$, $B(-1,4)$, and $C(0,-5)$ across all axes.

A. Reflection along y-axis

Point A: $\begin{bmatrix} -1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -2 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix}$ → A'(-2, 3)
Point B: $\begin{bmatrix} -1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix}$ → B'(1, 4)
Point C: $\begin{bmatrix} -1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ -5 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ -5 \end{bmatrix}$ → C'(0, -5)

B. Reflection along x-axis

Point A: $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix}$ → A''(2, -3)
Point B: $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix}$ → B''(-1, -4)
Point C: $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ -5 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 5 \end{bmatrix}$ → C''(0, 5)

C. Reflection along line $y=x$

Point A: $\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}$ → A'''(3, 2)
Point B: $\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix}$ → B'''(4, -1)
Point C: $\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ -5 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -5 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}$ → C'''(-5, 0)

D. Reflection along line $y=-x$

Point A: $\begin{bmatrix} 0 & -1 \\ -1 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -3 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix}$ → A''''(-3, -2)
Point B: $\begin{bmatrix} 0 & -1 \\ -1 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -4 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}$ → B''''(-4, 1)
Point C: $\begin{bmatrix} 0 & -1 \\ -1 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ -5 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}$ → C''''(5, 0)

3. Triangle Enlargement (Prob 23)

Triangle $A(-2,3), B(3,3), C(3,5)$ mapped by scale factor $k = -2$.

Rule: Multiply coordinates by factor $k$.
Matrix: $\begin{bmatrix} -2 & 0 \\ 0 & -2 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \end{bmatrix}$.
Vertex A(-2, 3): $(-2 \cdot -2, -2 \cdot 3) = (4, -6)$
Vertex B(3, 3): $(-2 \cdot 3, -2 \cdot 3) = (-6, -6)$
Vertex C(3, 5): $(-2 \cdot 3, -2 \cdot 5) = (-6, -10)$
